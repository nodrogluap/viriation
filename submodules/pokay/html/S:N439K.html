SARS-CoV-2 Spike protein (S) gene S:p.N439K literature reference collection

ACE2 receptor binding affinity

  • Enhances RBD:hACE2 affinity approximately 2-fold.
    Observed that the N439K mutation results in viral fitness (culture-based) that is similar or possibly slightly improved relative to the wild-type N439 virus (Thomson et al. (2021))

Antibody epitope effects

  • Decreases in neutralization efficiency up to ~25-50% in the S2D97 mAb.

  • Decreases in neutralization efficiency up to ~15-125% in the REGN10987 mAb.
    (Thomson et al. (2020))
  • Resistent to neutralizing mAb H00S022 (Li et al. (2020))
  • 6.8% of 442 tested post-infection sera showed a greater than two-fold reduction in binding to N439K RBD as compared to WT. In some individuals, the >two-fold reduction diminished the RBD ED50 response below 30 a threshold previously determined to be a cutoff for specific binding (Thomson et al. (2021))
  • Ablates binding by class 3 mAbs such as C135 that do not directly interfere with ACE2 binding, but clonal somatic mutations of memory B cells at 6.2 months (evolving humoral immune response) show pronounced increase in binding to the variant.
    (Gaebler et al. (2021))
  • Resistent to class 3 antibodies (i.e. Abs that do not directly interfere with ACE2 binding).
    (Wang et al. (2021))

Convalescent plasma escape

  • Average ~5-fold reduction in neutralization efficacy in convalescent sera of 16 health workers infected in Spring 2020.
    (Alenquer et al. (2021))
  • Observed ~2x decrease on average in neutralization efficacy in convalescent sera of 16 health workers.
    (Alenquer et al. (2021))
  • Resistant to a pool of 10 convalescent sera (but less than 4x, a typical threshold for definition of escape) (Li et al. (2020))

Homoplasy

  • Mutation has arisen independently multiple times, twice forming significant lineages (Thomson et al. (2021))
