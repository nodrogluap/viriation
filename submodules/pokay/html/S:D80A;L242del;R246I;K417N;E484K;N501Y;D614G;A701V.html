  • During an outbreak of SARS-CoV-2 501Y.V2 in a nursing home, all non-vaccinated residents (5/5) versus half of those fully vaccinated 2-5 weeks prior with BNT162b2 (13/26) were infected. Two of 13 vaccinated versus 4 of 5 non-vaccinated residents presented severe disease. BNT162b2 did not prevent the outbreak, but reduced transmission and disease severity. Among the 13 fully vaccinated residents who were infected, 2 (15.4%) presented with an asymptomatic disease, 9 (69.2%) developed mild to moderate symptoms, and 2 (15.4%) progressed to severe disease with fatal evolution secondary to acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS). There was no relationship between anti-S antibody levels at diagnosis and disease severity.
