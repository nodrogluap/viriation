Convalescent plasma binding

  • In sera from six patients collected 1 to 12 weeks post-infection with B.1, antibody titer in a microneutralization assay was on average 8 for B.1.351 virus.
    Compare to much stronger neutralization titers for B.1.1.7 (256) and wild type (456.1) in the same sera. (Luftig et al. (2021))
  • Sera from individuals who have been infected with Delta does not have strong neutralising activity against Beta.

Pharmaceutical effectiveness

  • B.1.351 virus neutralization (as tested using biolayer interferometry) of both Lilly antibodies was severely impacted, with LY-CoV16 and LY-CoV555 (due to mutations at 416 and 484 respectively) shows almost complete loss of neutralization of B.1.351.
    There was also escape from neutralization of B.1.351 by REGN10933 and a modest reduction in neutralization of B.1.351 by AZD8895 (while AZD1061 and AZD7442 showed equal neutralization of all SARS-CoV-2 variants tested). The three Adagio antibodies neutralized B.1.351.
    (Dejnirattisai et al. (2021))

Vaccinee plasma binding

  • Sera from individuals who have been vaccinated and have had subsequent recent Delta infection have a high level of neutralising activity against Beta.
