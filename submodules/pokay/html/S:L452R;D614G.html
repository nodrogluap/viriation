SARS-CoV-2 Spike protein (S) gene S:p.L452R;D614G literature reference collection

ACE2 receptor binding affinity

  • Using flow cytometry and ACE2 ectodomains-Fc portion IgG complex, this variant showed a 2.66x increase in binding (KD) relative to D614G.
    (Gong et al. (2021))

Convalescent plasma binding

  • 2.15x increase in Spike binding (relative to D614G alone) by 5 plasma collected 8 months post-symptom-onset.
    (Gong et al. (2021))

Convalescent plasma escape

  • Relative to B.1, Epsilon (B.1.417/429) shows 1.74x-2.35x decrease in neutralization efficiency by convalescent plasma [no cohort details provided] (Wilhelm et al. (2021))

Transmissibility

  • Increased infectivity of the B.1.617 spike was attributed to L452R, which itself caused a 3.5-fold increase in infectivity relative to D614G wild type.
    [In combination with E484Q caused a somewhat less 3-fold increase] (Tada et al. (2021))
  • The L452R mutation increased the infectivity more than two-fold in these conditions.
    (Mishra et al. (2021))

Vaccine efficacy

  • In a 1:5 test-negative matched control study of 8153 individuals who received the Moderna mRNA-1273 vaccine, 583 test-positive were Epsilon.
    Two dose vaccine efficacy against Epsilon was 97.6 (90.2-99.4%), one dose VE was 76.3 (48.1-89.1%). (Bruxvoort et al. (2021))

Vaccine neutralization efficacy

  • Using a pseudovirus assay and 6 ChAdOx1 vaccinee sera, this RBD mutation conferred a 2.36x drop in neutralization (NT50).
    (Mishra et al. (2021))
  • Relative to B.1, Epsilon (B.1.417/429) shows 1.74x-2.35x decrease in neutralization efficiency by 18 vaccinee sera (BNT162b2 and mRNA1273).
    (Wilhelm et al. (2021))

Vaccinee plasma binding

  • 1.05x decrease in Spike binding (relative to D614G alone) by 5 plasma collected 3 weeks after one dose of Pfizer/BioNtech BNT162b2 vaccine in previously non-seroconverted subjects.
    1.16x decrease in Spike binding (relative to D614G alone) by 5 plasma collected 3 weeks after one dose of Pfizer/BioNtech BNT162b2 vaccine in post-infection vaccinees.
    (Gong et al. (2021))
