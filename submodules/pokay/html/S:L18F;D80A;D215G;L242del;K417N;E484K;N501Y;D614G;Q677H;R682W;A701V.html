Vaccine neutralization efficacy

  • Pzifer vaccinees that tested positive at least a week after the second dose were indeed disproportionally infected with B.1.351, as compared with unvaccinated individuals (odds ratio of 8:1), but were all infected before 14 days post second vaccination.
    (Kustin et al. (2021))
  • Monovalent mRNA-1273.351 encodes for the S protein found in the B.1.351 lineage and (2) mRNA-1273.211 comprising a 1:1 mix of mRNA-1273 and mRNA-1273.351.
    In a mouse study, primary vaccination series of mRNA-1273.351 was effective at increasing neutralizing antibody titers against the B.1.351 lineage, while mRNA-1273.211 was most effective at providing broad cross-variant neutralization. In addition, these results demonstrated a third dose of mRNA-1273.351 significantly increased both wild-type and B.1.351-specific neutralization titers.
    (Wu et al. (2021))
  • Four fold reduction in neutralization efficiency was observed in sera of ferrets post-vaccination with INO-4800 (DNA plasmid pGX9501 encoding full length Spike).
    (Riddell et al. (2021))
  • In human sera 8 weeks post-vaccination with INO-4800, a ~7-fold reduction in B.1.351 neutralization was observed.
    (Andrade et al. (2021))
